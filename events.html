CUBE presents Swan Lake Fall 2018

Performances

November 8th and 9th at 8:00PM and Nov 11th at 2:00PM
Manhattan Movement & Arts Center, 248 W 60th St

Columbia University Ballet Ensemble is proud to present its Fall 2018 production of Swan Lake. Founded in 2013, Columbia University Ballet Ensemble (CUBE) is an inclusive dance group comprised of students from Barnard College and Columbia University. Every semester, the student members of CUBE plan, produce, and perform a full length story ballet. We seek to provide all of our members with the opportunity to learn and perform roles that are challenging and appropriate for their level of experience.

This November, Columbia University Ballet Ensemble presents Swan Lake, one of the most iconic, romantic, and tragic classical ballets. Swan Lake tells the story of a Prince named Siegfried and his quest to find love. On his birthday, his mother gifts him with a crossbow. She also tells him that he is now old enough to marry a princess. He heads out to hunt lake with his friend Benno. There, he encounters a beautiful White Swan, Odette, and falls madly in love. He quickly realizes, however, that Odile has been cursed by an evil sorcerer named Rothbart. He returns to his palace to tell his mother that he is in love, but she has already arranged a ball, featuring many beautiful princesses, that he must attend. Before the ball is over, Rothbart shows up with his daughter, the Black Swan Odile, who is nearly identical to Odette. The wicked sorcerer uses Odile to trick the Prince into declaring his love for her. The Prince soon agrees to marry Odile; as soon as he does, it is revealed that Odile is not the White Swan but an imposter. The White Swan Odette and the Prince are heartbroken, and Rothbart's curse separates them forever. The masterpiece Swan Lake comes to an end, concluding in tragedy.
