Columbia University Ballet Ensemble is proud to present its Spring 2018 production of Coppélia. Founded in 2013, Columbia University Ballet Ensemble (CUBE) is an inclusive dance group comprised of students from Barnard College and Columbia University. Every semester, the student members of CUBE plan, produce, and perform a full length story ballet. We seek to provide all of our members with the opportunity to learn and perform roles that are challenging and appropriate for their level of experience.

This semester, CUBE is excited to present the beloved Coppélia. Coppélia is a family-friendly and whimsical ballet that tells the story of the young couple Franz and Swanhilda. Early in the ballet, Franz is smitten with a mysterious young woman named Coppélia, who lives in the home of the mysterious Dr. Coppelius. Swanhilda discovers Franz's flirtations and storms off, furious. Later that night, Franz and Swanhilda separately sneak into Dr. Coppélius' home to learn more about Coppélia. Inside, they are shocked to discover a workshop full of life-sized, dancing dolls! Dr. Coppélius returns home abruptly. Chaos ensues as Swanhilda tries to save Franz from Dr. Coppelius, Dr. Coppelius tries to bring his dolls to life, and Franz tries to escape with Swanhilda. The ballet concludes with a triumphant wedding scene.
